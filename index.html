About Me

Hi, I am Ahammad Ullah. I recently received my undergraduate degree from the department of Materials & Metallurgical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering & technology (BUET). Over the last year of my undergrad, I was heavily involved with several computational research projects in the field of Transition Metal Dichalcogenide (TMDs) ex. WSe2, Transition Metal Oxides (TMOs) ex. CuO, and V2O5 Cathode Battery. These projects were carried out through collaboration with the research group of Dr. Saquib Ahmed from SUNY Buffalo University, USA and Dr. Md. Muktadir Billah from my university. I modelled & investigated the Cl-incorporated CuO anode materials with enhanced conductivity for next-generation Li-ion batteries using density functional theory. I also collaborated on a molecular dynamics simulation of V2O5 as a cathode material for Li-ion batteries project and I will also investigate the voltage and intercalation properties. My work on these projects was driven by a passion for exploring the intersection between material science & sustainable energy storage. During my PhD studies, I wish to continue to investigate these material systems & expand my research through first principles/data-driven computational studies along with experimental synthesis and characterization techniques. I have a keen interest in exploring stability and degradation of these types of materials/devices and work towards removing barriers to their commercialization.

Projects Overview

I have a keen interest in understanding the electronic & atomistic structures of a material & correlating its properties & device scale performance. The research I have been involved with has touched upon the following three areas of study:

1. Density Functional Theory.

  • Modeling the lattices of TMOs such as CuO
  • Implementing schemes for increasing the accuracy of band gap prediction
  • Optical Calculation such as dielectric function, absorption, reflectance etc.
  • DFT with U correction
  • Spin polarized DFT for antiferromagnetic system

    • 2. Density Functional Perturbation Theory and Phonopy.

  • Modeling the phonon calculation
  • Implementing phonopy for post processing of DFPT output.
  • Studying the the phonon spectrum and understanding the acoustic and optical branches
  • Phonon partial density of states for each atom and thermodynamic properties

    • 3. WSe2 Image Segmentation

  • Pixel extraxtion of red, green, blue (RGB) channel from WSe2flakes optical images mechanically exfoliated onto a Si substrate
  • Preprocessed to exclude the background effect and analyzed using the pixel medians and interquartile ranges for RGB channel
  • Thresholds were established for each channel for comparison of each channel's performance
  • The intersection-over-union (IoU) revealed red and green channels demonstrated greater than 99% and 90% accuracy in differentiating each layer
  • Overcome the requirement of large dataset of for conventional machine learning methodology

    • 4. Molecular Dynamics Simulation of V2O5 cathode Battery. (Current Project)

  • Atomistic modeling of cathode and three electrode cell using Packmol
  • Structure equilibration using appropriate ensembles
  • Evaluating Cohesive & Adsorption energies
  • Modeling the proper mixture of 1 M Na2SO4 electrolyle
  • Further Identify the voltage, charging-discharging cycle, intercalation in future
